Genesys EMEA Partners PureCloud Hackathon

Extending PureCloud Beyond Contact Centers

O Hackathon

It is with great excitement that Genesys EMEA in partnership with Amazon announces the Genesys EMEA PureCloud Partners Hackathon during the EMEA Advanced Partner Summit. The hackathon will start on the 22nd of October until the 25th.

Entries are open to all Genesys PureCloud partners and must be made through our registration form. Throughout the hackathon, the Genesys team will be giving technical support, mentoring and ideation.

Challenge: Build an app for PureCloud to help improve the day-to-day experience customers have, using features of the PureCloud API and/or an API of any external service of your choice (as long as it's related to contact centers and PureCloud).

Early Access: You will get early access to some beta features. These features will be enabled for your org shortly before the hackathon (hopefully, the chat API everyone has been so eager to play with!).

Access to Dev: We will also bring 1 or 2 PureCloud developers from the US to assist you and your team. A room will be set up to also talk to other developers remotely in a once in a lifetime opportunity for you, our partners!

Prizes: Prizes are still to be determined however, let me tell you this: they will definitely be worth the visit! We are still in the process of bringing in some sponsors. The number of prizes will depend on the number of teams attending this event so join now!

Rules

All teams must have at least 3 people. If you do not have enough members in your team, please send me an email so I can put you in touch with other lonely developers

All team members have to register individually by filling in this form before being able to participate.

Each team member must sign a "Hackathon Participant Agreement" which will be provided after registration. This agreement will list the legal conditions of this hackathon to settle any legal disputes (and because our legal department does not give us any choice).

In order for your project to be presented in the demo session, the developed app must be published in a source code repository that can be accessed by the judges. Code will be reviewed either before and during the presentation(s).

All teams will have to present a demo of their solution. The presentation will be 8 minutes + 2 minutes of jury Q&A session.

The use of existing code is forbidden! Everyone will start the programming marathon at the same time. This ensures impartiality of the competition. Do not participate in the competition if you plan on re-using an existing project.

Scoring

Projects will be evaluated with the following criteria:

Category Percent Total Score Description
Innovation 50% Is it a unique solution to an existing problem? Does it replace an existing solution? If so, how is it an improvement?
Cool Factor 25% Forget all the practical stuff... how cool is it?
Business Potential/ROI 10% Does the application create value? Is it marketable? Will customers pay for it? What is the ROI?
Presentation 10% How clearly were the problem and solution described? How professionally was it presented?
User Experience 5% Does it have an attractive, easy-to-use interface?
